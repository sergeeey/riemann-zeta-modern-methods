## CAVEAT-1 — 1/(48π) is a tautology, not emergence  → ACCEPTED [VERIFIED-tool]
weyl_err·T → 1/(48π) because that is literally the 3rd term of the Stirling
expansion of θ(T). n_BK = first 2 terms; residual = 3rd term. Confirmed to
T=500000 (rel 0.0000%). Do NOT present as a nontrivial match.

## CAVEAT-2 — "natural candidate" is not operationally defined  → ACCEPTED
A density match constrains H very weakly — ANY system with logarithmic level
density gives the same leading count. Density match is the #1 false-proof pattern
for RH. This experiment shows xp passes a NECESSARY (not sufficient) check.

## CAVEAT-3 — "std persists" is not statistically significant  → ACCEPTED
0.40 vs 0.34 over 50 points: F=1.38 < F_crit 1.98. Noise. Correct claim: S(T)
behavior is consistent with Selberg's √(lnlnT/2π²); the T-range [50,2500] is too
narrow to observe the (very slow) growth.

## CAVEAT-4 — results are not new  → ACCEPTED
- Density-only match: Berry & Keating themselves stated this in 1999.
- ⟨S⟩=0, std>0: Selberg 1946.
- wrong-norm grows: trivial dimensional scaling, not physics.
The genuine value here is internal: hands-on confirmation of WHERE xp breaks,
and calibration of our toolkit against textbook results — NOT a contribution.

## CAVEAT-5 — wrong-norm control is weak  → ACCEPTED
ℓxℓp=π failing is just choice-of-units, not evidence that 2π is physically
required. Keep it only as a sanity check, not as support for the claim.

## What was genuinely useful (honest positives)
- S vs independent arg ζ agree to 0.00000 → our S is not a circular artifact of nzeros.

## What this experiment does NOT mean

2. Does NOT prove RH (S(T), where RH lives, is not reproduced — by construction).
3. Is NOT a new result (re-derives Berry-Keating 1999 + Selberg 1946).
4. The "why xp is natural" reading must be downgraded to "xp passes one necessary check".
